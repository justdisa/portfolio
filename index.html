Who uses your website?

Everyone can picture a typical web user. A millennial, probably: under thirty and technically literate, glued to an iPhone. But the reality is very different. The Pew Research Center tells us that 88% of US adults are online, and this includes 64% of those over the age of sixty-five. Those numbers are climbing. As the Baby Boom ages, they'll climb even faster.

Further, according to a United States Census Bureau report, 19% of Americans have a disability, and half of those consider it severe. Any designer who neglects this significant minority of users will fall behind one who doesn't.

And yet most of us are still designing with a young, able-bodied user in mind.
